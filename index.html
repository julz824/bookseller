ABOUT US


The bookstore "McReady's" was created by inspiration and great love for creativity and writers' art. The main task of the store is to attract the younger generation and instill a love for books. The name "McReady's" was taken in honor of William Charles Macready, an English writer and actor, a talented performer of classical and domestic roles, with great success playing in London, Paris, the USA. In Shakespeare's plays, he was better able to perform roles that required finer finishes than heroic types. According to Lewis, he was inimitable in the roles of Iago, Richard II, Shylock, etc.; no one gave Lyre's senile irritability with such perfection. In gentle scenes, as well as in scenes that arouse compassion in the audience, he had no rivals. In 1851, he made his last public appearance in the role of Macbeth. After his death, he published his interesting memoirs "Reminiscences, and Selections from His Diaries and Letters" (London, 1875), which can be found at the main counter of the McReady's bookstore. Founded in 2018 the McReady's bookstore differs from the rest of the industry in its unique concept. Several rooms have been opened not only for the purpose of selling books (domestic and foreign publishers), but also with the function of the library, rest and reading of any books of interest to visitors. There is also a hall with an author's coffee house, a hall for public readings, various literary, theater events and lectures. Thus, the "McReady's" turns into a conceptual space and community for people close to the spirit who are in love with art and literature.
